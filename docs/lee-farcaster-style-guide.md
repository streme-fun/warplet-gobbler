# Lee Knowlton Farcaster Style Guide

Generated with Spiral from Lee's Farcaster cast samples.

Data source: Neynar fetched 486 non-empty root casts for fid `446697` (root
casts only, `include_replies=false`), ranked by engagement score
`likes + 3*recasts + 2*replies`. I selected 84 high-signal samples spanning
top performers, recent posts, and ordinary baseline posts, then sent a trimmed
sample set to Spiral. Top examples included the WarpletGobbler launch, Streme
Pulse shipping note, Farcaster Ecosystem for Agents announcement, personal
crypto/Farcaster reflection, Monad staking mini app, and short "Who's making
this?" questions.

## 1. Core Voice Traits

Lee's voice balances builder transparency with community warmth.

- Direct and unfiltered: says what he means without corporate softening.
- First-person dominant: uses "I," "we," and "my" frequently; product and
  protocol posts still feel personal.
- Playful and serious in the same feed: can move from protocol risk to "y'all
  are animals" without the shift feeling forced.
- Community-focused: names specific people, celebrates others' work, and builds
  in public with the room.
- Concrete over abstract: backs claims with numbers, links, product names, or
  specific examples.
- Comfortable with short posts: some casts are complete in a phrase when the
  surrounding context is already understood.

## 2. What High-Engagement Casts Have In Common

The strongest casts tend to have:

- Mechanism clarity first: when introducing a game, product, or system, the
  "how to play" or "how it works" section is scannable and testable. Readers
  can understand the action and the outcome without needing to click through.
- Grounded specificity: high-engagement personal posts start with a concrete
  detail before zooming out. Product posts name the thing, the numbers, and the
  mechanism instead of leaning on vague hype.
- Metrics and movement: posts that show holdings, auction stats, floor price,
  token movement, or shipped status make progress visible.
- Brevity with context: very short posts can work when the conversation is
  already live in the feed.
- Builder or community flavor: posts that name collaborators, credit other
  builders, or celebrate community outcomes feel native to Farcaster.
- Minimal marketing voice: prefer "shipped," "launched," "just dropped," or a
  direct conversational stake over polished corporate phrasing.

Neynar data confirms the guide's core thesis, but sharpens one point:
mechanism clarity and visible metrics matter more than the first pass stressed.
The best posts explain how to play or what changed with enough specificity that
readers get the gist immediately. Personal warmth and builder-community focus
remain central, but they work best when anchored in concrete detail or visible
movement.

## 3. Common Post Structures

### Product Launch

```text
[Human need or outcome hook].

Introducing: [Product Name] [optional emoji]

- [Feature 1]: [specific description]
- [Feature 2]: [specific description]
- [Feature 3]: [specific description, with perk if relevant]

[Optional link or next step]
```

### Mechanic Or System Explainer

```text
[Topic + what is happening]

--HOW TO PLAY--
1. [Action] -> [outcome]
or
2. [Alternative action] -> [outcome]

--HOW IT WORKS--
[Clear explanation of the mechanism]

[Optional result, stat, or link]
```

### Lightweight Update

```text
[Action/result] + [small emotional beat]
```

Examples:

- `update: merged and didn't break anything`
- `oh SNAP, we're done!`
- `goodbye fc pro`

### Personal Reflection

```text
[Grounded personal anecdote or observation].

[Larger thought, joke, or simple conclusion].
```

The post should start from a real detail, not from a general life lesson.

## 4. Hooks That Fit Lee

Use hooks that are specific, concrete, or lightly provocative.

- Question hook: "Have you ever wanted to design & order your own custom merch?"
- Time/context hook: "Today Streme is launching..."
- Contrast hook: "For thousands of years, people have had opinions. But..."
- Personal hook: "Every year my wife and I surprise each other..."
- Counter-narrative hook: "They may tell you Farcaster is dying, but..."
- Minimal hook: a short phrase when the context already exists.

Avoid polished marketing openers like "We're thrilled to announce..."

## 5. Rhythm And Formatting

- Use line breaks as structure.
- Keep most paragraphs to one or two sentences.
- Use bullets when listing features, benefits, or stats.
- Use numbered lists when explaining how to play or how something works.
- Put links at the end or on their own line.
- Do not overpack casts. Let the post breathe.

Lee's rhythm often alternates between:

- a short declarative line,
- a blank line,
- a concrete explanation or list,
- then a small aside or CTA.

## 6. Humor And Wit

Lee's humor is embedded, not forced.

- Dry understatement works.
- Community in-jokes work when the audience already has the context.
- Self-aware absurdity works when tied to a real situation.
- Light profanity is acceptable when it matches the moment.
- The joke should feel like a side effect of honesty, not the main goal.

Do not bolt on jokes after writing. If the subject is not funny, be clear
instead.

## 7. Punctuation And Capitalization

- Mostly sentence case.
- All caps only for acronyms, protocols, tokens, or deliberate section labels.
- Exclamation marks are rare and should feel earned.
- Parentheses are useful for asides or clarifications.
- Ellipses are rare.
- Headers may use the double-dash pattern: `--HOW TO PLAY--`.
- Emoji should be sparse, usually zero to two per cast, and should mark a real
  tone shift or visual cue.

## 8. Product Update Style

When writing product updates:

1. Start with the user outcome or product moment.
2. Name the thing clearly.
3. Explain the mechanism in plain language.
4. Use concrete stats when available.
5. Give credit to collaborators or community when relevant.
6. Include a link or next action if there is one.
7. Keep the tone confident but not salesy.

Good product-update posture:

```text
[Thing changed]

[Why it matters]

[Specific numbers / mechanics / user impact]

[What is next]
```

For WarpletGobbler-style updates, the useful ingredients are:

- current holdings,
- auction/top-bid stats,
- floor or token movement,
- what changed in the game loop,
- why the change improves incentives.

## 9. Personal And Reflection Style

Personal casts should be specific before they are philosophical.

Good pattern:

```text
[Small concrete moment]

[Observation that lets others see themselves in it]
```

Avoid:

- generic advice,
- over-explaining the emotion,
- turning every personal moment into a lesson,
- writing as if it is a LinkedIn post.

The best reflective casts are warm, brief, and grounded in a real scene.

## 10. Do Rules

- Be specific.
- Put the payoff early.
- Use numbers, names, links, and concrete mechanisms.
- Credit builders and collaborators.
- Keep product posts scannable.
- Ask real questions when you want input.
- Use short posts when context is already known.
- Let humor come from the situation.
- Say what changed and why it matters.
- Preserve a sense of being in the Farcaster room, not broadcasting at it.

## 11. Don't Rules

- Do not write generic marketing copy.
- Do not say "we're excited to announce" unless there is a very specific reason.
- Do not over-explain obvious context.
- Do not force jokes.
- Do not overuse emoji.
- Do not hide the action or link.
- Do not turn personal posts into motivational posters.
- Do not apologize for shipping.
- Do not use jargon without either shared context or a quick explanation.
- Do not pretend uncertainty is certainty.

```text

```

### Shipping Note

```text

```

### Personal Reflection

```text
[Specific personal scene].

[Small conclusion, joke, or observation].
```

### Community Signal

```text
[Observation about what people are doing]

[Why it matters / what it says about the community]
```

## 13. Pre-Post Checklist

Before casting:

- Is the core value clear in the first two lines?
- Is the post written for Farcaster, not a press release?
- Is there a concrete detail, number, name, or link?
- Are the line breaks doing useful work?
- If there is a list, is each bullet specific?
- If there is a joke, does it come from the situation?
- If it is a product update, can a reader understand what changed?
- If it is personal, does it start with a real moment?
- Did I remove generic hype language?
- Does it sound like Lee would actually cast it?

## 14. Future Neynar Workflow

The Neynar workflow is operational and should be the recurring refresh process.

1. Set `NEYNAR_API_KEY` in the environment.
2. Fetch root casts for fid `446697` with `include_replies=false`.
3. Pull reactions, recasts, replies, timestamps, text, and channel context.
   Pull views/impressions too if the selected endpoint exposes them.
4. Score engagement:

```text
score = likes + (3 * recasts) + (2 * replies)
```

5. Keep three buckets:
   - top performers,
   - recent posts, especially the last 30 days,
   - ordinary baseline posts that anchor what "normal" sounds like.
6. Feed all three buckets to Spiral for voice analysis and guide refresh.
7. Refresh this guide after major launches, or every 50-100 new casts.

The important part is not just collecting viral posts. The guide needs both
what resonates and what sounds like Lee in daily casts.
