```text
Yearly update on raising our son trilingual (English, Japanese, Chinese)

He's 3.5 now and is remarkably balanced between languages and very selective in what he speaks to who.

Background: 

Both parents (us) are experienced language educators and relatively well read in multilingual acquisition research, but it's always a new thing with your own children and, to an extent, each child is different. We want him to speak/read/write all three since they're all daily drivers for us.

Our Approach:

Mostly "one parent, one language" with a modification that we frequently talk about how speaking multiple languages is normal and cool. This is a defensive psyop for when he gets older and his peers question his multilingualism. No idea if it will work, but worth the try.

I speak English ~90% of the time with him and Japanese ~10% (tiny bit of Mandarin for fun contexts), usually in transition to English. He knows I speak both languages but defaults to speaking to me in English unless it's some kind of game straight from nursery school (e.g. クイズのクイズなんのクイズ / "Quiz! Quiz! What's the quiz?!").

Mom speaks 90% Mandarin to him, 5% English, 5% Japanese. He responds almost exclusively in Mandarin. 3yo knows that both he and mom are trilingual, and that dad is bilingual w/ a bit of Chinese.

We both read to him most evenings...sometimes in our normal languages, and sometimes in Japanese.

Nursery School is 100% Japanese and he doesn't have any multilingual peers (but does have multilingual friends outside of that).

How do we use technology?

Not much in terms of media. Average YouTube video time rounds is probably 10 minutes over the course of a month. Music is more common and I do let him sit on my lap as I code and study (and sometimes cast). I also let him play with Google Maps and call his grandparents on Signal.

What about reading and writing?

He can read all hiragana now, and some simple words in English (i.e. "The cat is hot"). We haven't done much in Mandarin yet but earlier on his grandparents did a bit. English literacy is very important to me even though we live in Japan because it will let him go beyond "just me" as a source of English.

Caveats:
He started speaking late and we've been getting extra support in Japanese to help his language development there. Also, I think his monolingual peers have stronger single language vocabularies, but he's more or less able to express as he desires in his language of choice.

Overall:
Very happy with how frequently he speaks and his language balance. Next year will likely incorporate more media, but I'm still very selective about it.
```
